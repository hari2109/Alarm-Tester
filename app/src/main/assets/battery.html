Disable Battery Optimization for this app. Find your device and follow the steps

  • Xiaomi:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open “Settings” and tap “Permissions”.
    2. Select “Autostart”.
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.
  • Battery Manager
    1. Open settings.
    2. Select “Battery & Performance”.
    3. Select “Choose Apps”.
    4. Find this app and select “No restrictions” in background settings.

 

 

  • Samsung:

 

  • Battery Manager
    1. Open “Settings” and tap “Device Maintenance”.
    2. Tap on Battery icon at bottom of the screen.
    3. Scroll down and Tap on Unmonitored apps.
    4. Tap on “Add apps”.
    5. Find this app and tap on it.
    6. Click on “Done”.

 

 

  • Oppo:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open Phone Manager and find Privacy Permissions.
    2. Enter Startup Manager
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.
  • Battery Manager
    1. Open settings and tap Battery.
    2. Tap energy saver.
    3. Find this app to turn off Background Freeze and Abnormal Apps Optimization.

                 If you can’t set after you complete the above steps, try to:

  1. Open Phone Manager and find Clean Background.
  2. Tap to add app.
  3. Tick this app and tap OK.

 

 

  • Vivo:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open i Manager and tap Permission Management
    2. In Permissions, find Autostart.
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.
  • Battery Manager
    1. Open Settings and tap Battery
    2. Select High background power consumption.
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.

 

 

  • Huawei:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open Phone Manager.
    2. Select and enter “Launch”
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.
    4. Tap to enter “Manage batch manually”.
    5. Find this app and tick “Auto-launch”, “Secondary Launch”, “Run in background”.

            If it doesn’t work after the above settings, try to:

  1. Open settings.
  2. Tap Permission manager.
  3. Tick this app and turn on the switch.

 

  • Battery Manager
    1. Open Settings.
    2. Select Advanced settings.
    3. Select Battery manager.
    4. Select Protected apps.
    5. Find this app and turn on the switch

If the EMUI version is updated recently, it’s unnecessary to make changes in Settings.

 

 

  • Le:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open Settings, find Privacy authorization.
    2. Select Autostart.
    3. Find this app and turn on the switch.

 

 

  • Meizu:

 

  • Autostart
    1. Open Security.
    2. Tap Permissions.
    3. Select App management.
    4. Find this app and turn on the switch.

 

 

  • Oneplus:

 

  • Battery optimizations:
    1. Open Settings.
    2. Find Battery, tap on it.
    3. Tap on Battery optimization.
    4. Find this app and click on it.
    5. Then select “Don’t optimise”

 

 

  • Motorola:

 

  • Battery optimizations:
    1. Open Settings.
    2. Find Battery, tap on it.
    3. Tap on Battery optimization (Either in list or top right corner menu).
    4. Change drop down (on top) to “All apps” (from Not optimised)
    5. Find this app and click on it.
    6. Then select “Don’t optimise”

 

 

  • Lenovo:

 

  • Battery optimizations:
    1. Open Settings.
    2. Find Battery/Power Manager, tap on it.
    3. Tap on Battery optimization.
    4. Change drop down (on top) to “All apps” (from Not optimised)
    5. Find this app and click on it.
    6. Then select “Don’t optimise”

 

 

  • Other Phone brands:

 

    1. Find Battery Optimization in settings, find this app and select “Don’t optimise”
    2. Lock/Keep this app in Recents.
    3. Allow this app to autostart in Security/Settings -> Autostart (if available)
    4. Add this app to exceptions.
    5. If user have installed another startup manager, remove this app from its blocklist.
